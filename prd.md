## The One-Line Truth

> *A student at UNILAG with an empty pocket has something to offer. Another student at the same university, with a full wallet but no time, needs exactly what that first student can give. ZBD is the bridge.*

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Zero Broke Days (ZBD) is Nigeria's first **campus-native, anything-goes ethical task marketplace** — built exclusively for and around the university ecosystem. Unlike conventional gig platforms that focus on formal skills and professional services, ZBD embraces the full spectrum of student need and student capability: from writing lab reports and tutoring to washing plates, checking if there's light at a hostel, filling seats at an embarrassingly empty event, or helping someone move a mattress to a new room.

The platform's founding thesis is radical in its simplicity: **at any university in Nigeria, wealth and poverty sit side by side in the same lecture hall.** ZBD monetises that proximity. The rich student who needs things done, the poor student who needs income — they are already on the same campus. We are just the connection.

Starting at the University of Lagos (UNILAG), ZBD will expand systematically to every major university in Nigeria, building the largest campus-specific micro-economy in Africa.

## 2. The Real Problem

### 2.1 The Surface Problem
Nigerian students face financial hardship. School fees, feeding, data, transportation, and textbooks all compete for money that often doesn't exist.

### 2.2 The Deeper Problem
The tools that exist to solve this are built for the wrong person, in the wrong place, for the wrong kind of work.

- **Conventional job platforms** (LinkedIn, Jobberman) require polished CVs, formal experience, and long hiring cycles. A 200-Level student who needs ₦1,500 for data today cannot use these.
- **Freelance platforms** (Fiverr, Upwork) require skills, portfolios, and payment systems that many students don't have.
- **Existing gig platforms** (Bolt Food, Jumia Food delivery) require assets — bikes, cars — or restrict work to a narrow category.
- **Physical noticeboards** exist on campuses but are informal, unsearchable, untrusted, and unpaid in cash.

### 2.3 What Nobody Has Solved
Nobody has built a platform that says: **"Any task. Any price. Any skill. As long as it is ethical and legal — it belongs here."**

Nobody has acknowledged that the most desperate need a student has today might not be "I need a graphic designer." It might be:
- "I need someone to stand in queue at the bursary for 4 hours while I attend class."
- "I need to know if the generator is on at Block D before I carry all these things from Block A."
- "My event has 6 people and I invited 80. I need bodies in seats in 2 hours."
- "I haven't washed plates in 3 weeks and I need this room clean before my parents visit tomorrow morning."

These tasks are real. They happen every day on every campus. Nobody is monetising them. ZBD will.

## 3. Vision

**Zero Broke Days is the economic nervous system of the Nigerian university campus.**

We are not building a gig app. We are building a new informal economy that is:

- **Embedded** in campus culture
- **Trusted** because verifiable through student identity
- **Accessible** to any student regardless of formal skills
- **Dignifying** — any honest work is respected work
- **Transformative** — over time, a student who starts by washing plates can build a reputation, earn ratings, discover what they're good at, and move up the gig ladder within the same platform

The long-term vision is a campus where no motivated student should ever be broke, and no student who needs help should ever be unable to get it.

## 4. The ZBD Thesis — What Makes This Different

### 4.1 The "Any Gig" Policy
ZBD's most radical and defensible product decision is this: **we do not gatekeep task categories.**

Most platforms are embarrassed by small, informal, or "undignified" tasks. ZBD is not. We celebrate them. A student who earns ₦500 to run an errand is not less than one who earns ₦5,000 tutoring. Both transactions happen because of ZBD, and both are valid, tracked, and protected.

**Examples of valid ZBD gigs:**
| Category | Example Gig |
|---|---|
| Academic | Write lab report for CHE221 |
| Academic | Take thorough notes in MTH302 (I'm traveling) |
| Academic | Proofread and edit my final year project chapter |
| Errand | Run to GTBank ATM and confirm if it has cash |
| Errand | Check if there's light at Amina Hostel before I come back |
| Errand | Queue for clearance forms at the Registrar's office |
| Manual | Wash 3 days of plates in Room 14B |
| Manual | Help me move my things from Block A to Block C |
| Manual | Set up chairs and tables for departmental dinner |
| Social | Attend my event (just come, sit, look engaged) |
| Social | Be my plus-one to a formal dinner (must be well-dressed) |
| Social | Celebrate someone's birthday with me — need 10 people |
| Creative | Design my departmental flyer in Canva |
| Creative | Take photos at my convocation ceremony |
| Technical | Fix this Python bug in 30 minutes |
| Technical | Help me set up my laptop before exams |
| Convenience | Buy me 2 plates of jollof rice from SUB and bring to my room |
| Convenience | Go to the pharmacy in Akoka and get these items for me |
| Niche | Record yourself delivering a motivational speech as a gift for my friend |

### 4.2 Campus Verifiability = Trust At Scale
Every user is verified by student ID or NYSC certificate. This single fact changes everything. When you know the person offering or taking a gig is physically on your campus, verifiable through their school ID, and living within 500 metres of you — trust is structurally embedded, not hoped for.

This is ZBD's unfair advantage over any national gig platform. We don't need to build trust from scratch. The campus already provides it. We just have to capture it.

### 4.3 Proximity Engine — "Near Me Now"
ZBD gigs are not just posted into a void. They are mapped to:
- **Hostel / Hall of Residence** (Amina, Moremi, Fabian, etc.)
- **Faculty / Department**
- **Campus Zone** (Main Gate axis, SUB axis, Yaba Gate axis)
- **Time sensitivity** (Right now / Today / This week)

A gig posted by someone in Amina Hostel surfaces first to people in Amina and nearby. A task in the Engineering faculty shows up for Engineering students first. Speed is everything — and proximity enables speed.

### 4.4 The Equality Engine
ZBD's social thesis is explicit: **the platform systematically transfers economic opportunity from high-income to low-income students, within the same campus, without charity.**

The rich student doesn't give a handout. They get a real service done. The less-privileged student doesn't receive charity. They earn money for real work. Both parties gain. The transaction is dignified on both sides.

Over time, this compounds: the student who starts with errands builds a profile, earns reviews, discovers skills they didn't know were marketable, and climbs into higher-paying gig tiers — all within ZBD.

## 6. User Personas

### 6.1 The Hustler — "Tunde"
- **Who:** 200-Level student, public university, comes from a low-income background. Scholarship only covers school fees. Feeding and data are on him.
- **Pain:** ₦0 in his wallet on a Tuesday. Exam on Thursday. Needs to eat, needs data to download past questions.
- **ZBD use:** Checks the app. Sees a posting — "₦800 to queue for clearance form at Registrar, 9am." He goes. By 10am he has ₦800. By Friday he's done 4 gigs and has ₦3,200. Exam bought.
- **What he needs from ZBD:** Speed. Simplicity. No hoops. Cash the same day.

### 6.2 The Seeker — "Amaka"
- **Who:** 400-Level student, parents are comfortable. She's busy with project work, internship applications, and social life.
- **Pain:** She needs things done that she doesn't have time or energy for. She has money but no mechanism to safely delegate small tasks on campus.
- **ZBD use:** Posts "₦1,500 — someone please bring me 2 wraps and puff-puff from SUB. I'm in the Law Library." Gets a taker in 6 minutes.
- **What she needs from ZBD:** Trust. Reliability. Easy posting. Reviews.

### 6.3 The Academic Specialist — "Emeka"
- **Who:** 300-Level Engineering student with above-average grades. Understands courses deeply. Has free afternoons.
- **Pain:** His skills are worth money but he doesn't know how to market them. People come to him informally but he's never been paid.
- **ZBD use:** Lists himself as a CHE221 and MTH201 tutor at ₦2,000/hour. Gets 2–3 bookings a week. ₦15,000–25,000/month from tutoring alone.
- **What he needs from ZBD:** Discoverability. Schedule management. Payment protection.

### 6.4 The Event Organiser — "SUG President Kola"
- **Who:** Student Union executive, departmental event coordinator, club president.
- **Pain:** Events get posted, but attendance is embarrassing. He needs people in seats. He also needs setup crew, photographers, MCs.
- **ZBD use:** Posts "Attend MOSA Annual Dinner — ₦500 per head, smart casual, 7pm Saturday. 30 spots." Gets 28 takers by Friday afternoon.
- **What he needs from ZBD:** Group gig functionality. Attendance confirmation. Post-event rating.

### 6.5 The Campus Entrepreneur — "Fatimah"
- **Who:** Runs a small business — sells food, does hair, provides printing services from her room.
- **Pain:** Her customers only come if they already know about her. She has no reach outside her floor.
- **ZBD use:** Lists her services on ZBD. Gets discovered. Earns through the platform with payment protection.
- **What she needs from ZBD:** Business profile, service listing, campus-wide visibility, wallet.

## 7. Core Platform Features — MVP

### 7.1 Gig Posting
- Any registered user can post a gig
- Title, description, price (fixed or negotiable), location on campus, time sensitivity, spots available
- Category tagging (Academic, Errand, Manual, Creative, Technical, Social, Convenience, Other)
- Optional: Anonymous posting (for sensitive tasks)
- **"Any gig" policy** — no category restrictions beyond legality and ethics

### 7.2 Gig Discovery
- Home feed sorted by proximity + recency
- Category filters
- Campus-specific feed (only UNILAG gigs while at UNILAG)
- "Urgent" tag for time-sensitive postings
- Search by keyword, location, price range, category

### 7.3 Identity & Verification
- Student ID upload + selfie verification (manual review, <24hr turnaround for MVP)
- University email confirmation (e.g. @student.unilag.edu.ng)
- Profile displays: faculty, level, verified badge, rating, completed gigs count
- NYSC certificate accepted for fresh graduates

### 7.4 Trust & Safety System
*(See Section 8 for full detail)*

### 7.5 Wallet & Payments
- ZBD Wallet — each user has a campus wallet
- Gig payment held in escrow when a gig is accepted
- Released to worker upon completion confirmation by poster
- Minimum withdrawal: ₦500 (to reduce micro-transfer fees)
- Instant transfer to Nigerian bank accounts (Paystack/Flutterwave integration)
- Dispute window: 24 hours after completion — poster can raise a dispute

### 7.6 Matching & Application
- Worker browses and applies to a gig
- Poster sees applicant profiles (rating, completed gigs, verification status)
- Poster selects worker
- Chat opens between poster and worker (in-app messaging)
- Automated check-in: worker marks "Arrived," "In Progress," "Done"

### 7.7 Ratings & Reviews
- Both parties rate each other after gig completion (1–5 stars)
- Written review (optional)
- Review displayed publicly on profile
- Minimum 10 gigs before review manipulation becomes statistically insignificant (tracked)

### 7.8 Notifications
- Push + SMS for: new gig in your area, application accepted, payment received, new message, gig about to expire

## 8. Trust & Safety System

The campus context gives ZBD a structural advantage in trust, but this advantage must be actively maintained.

### 8.1 The Three Layers of Trust

**Layer 1 — Identity**
- Student ID verified before any transaction
- University email confirmed
- Selfie matched to student ID photo (manual or AI-assisted)
- Result: every user is a verified student of a specific institution

**Layer 2 — Reputation**
- Star ratings + written reviews accumulate with every gig
- Completion rate tracked (gigs accepted vs. gigs completed)
- Dispute history visible to ZBD admins
- "Top Hustler" badge for users with 50+ completed gigs, 4.5+ rating
- "Trusted Poster" badge for employers with 20+ gigs posted, no disputes

**Layer 3 — Community Reporting**
- Flag a gig as inappropriate, misleading, or potentially unsafe
- Report a user for misconduct
- Campus Safety Rep at each university (a trained student moderator)

### 8.2 Prohibited Content (The Ethics Boundary)
ZBD will accept any gig that is legal and ethical. The following are explicitly prohibited:

- Academic fraud: submitting work as your own that was done by another (posting allowed, but the use of that work for fraud is the student's responsibility — ZBD is not the academic integrity body of the university)
- Anything that is illegal under Nigerian law
- Anything involving physical or sexual harassment
- Gigs designed to humiliate, discriminate, or degrade
- Impersonation of another student in official academic settings
- Drug-related tasks

> **Note on "Write my lab report":** ZBD takes the position that it is not the platform's role to police how academic work is used. The student posts the gig, the student submits the work. ZBD facilitates a transaction between two people. The ethical responsibility of academic integrity lies with the poster, not the platform. This is the same standard applied to stationers who sell typeset papers.

### 8.3 Dispute Resolution
1. Worker marks gig complete
2. Poster has 24 hours to confirm or dispute
3. If disputed: ZBD support reviews chat history, check-ins, and both parties' statements
4. Resolution made within 48 hours
5. If worker is at fault: escrow returned to poster
6. If poster is at fault: escrow released to worker plus a 10% penalty deducted from poster's future gigs
7. Repeat bad actors are suspended

## 9. The Gig Taxonomy

ZBD recognises seven gig categories, intentionally designed to have no hierarchical order. A ₦300 errand is as valid as a ₦15,000 photography gig.

| # | Category | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Academic** | Anything related to academic life | Tutoring, note-taking, proofreading, slide design, past question compilation |
| 2 | **Errand** | Physical errands on or around campus | Queue substitution, information retrieval, item pickup, ATM/bank runs |
| 3 | **Manual** | Physical labour, setup, logistics | Event setup, room cleaning, plate washing, luggage moving, painting |
| 4 | **Creative** | Design, media, content | Flyer design, photography, videography, voice overs, social media content |
| 5 | **Technical** | Digital and technical skills | Coding help, laptop repair, WiFi setup, data analysis, app testing |
| 6 | **Social** | Presence, attendance, companionship | Event attendance, birthday celebrations, group outings, plus-one |
| 7 | **Convenience** | Anything that makes life easier | Food delivery on campus, room service, personal shopping, wakeup calls |

### 9.1 Pricing Guidelines (Not Enforced — Suggested)
| Gig Type | Suggested Range |
|---|---|
| Very quick errands (<30 min) | ₦200 – ₦800 |
| Standard tasks (1–2 hrs) | ₦800 – ₦3,000 |
| Skilled work (tutoring, design) | ₦2,000 – ₦10,000 |
| Full-day or event gigs | ₦5,000 – ₦20,000 |
| Specialist/rare skills | Market rate — negotiable |

Prices are set by the poster. Workers may negotiate in-app before accepting.

## 10. Financial Infrastructure

### 10.1 Payment Flow
```
Poster posts gig → Worker applies → Poster selects worker
    → Poster funds escrow (locks money in ZBD wallet)
        → Gig is executed → Worker marks complete
            → Poster confirms → Escrow released to worker
```

**Dispute path:**
```
... → Poster disputes → ZBD support reviews → Decision made → Funds released accordingly
```

### 10.2 ZBD Revenue Model
ZBD charges a **platform fee of 8–10%** on each successful gig transaction.

- This fee is charged to the poster (employer), not the worker.
- Rationale: the worker is often the economically vulnerable party. Taxing their earnings reduces the platform's social value proposition.
- At 9% average fee on ₦378M Year 1 GMV (UNILAG): **₦34M Year 1 revenue target.**

**Additional revenue streams (post-MVP):**
- **ZBD Pro for Posters:** ₦2,500/month for unlimited priority listings, featured placement, and analytics.
- **Campus Business Accounts:** Small campus businesses (printers, barbers, food vendors) pay ₦1,500/month to maintain a permanent business profile.
- **ZBD Credits:** Microtransaction currency for sub-₦200 gigs to reduce payment processing costs.
- **Referral Bonuses:** Funded by commission — refer 5 active users, earn ₦1,000 in ZBD Credits.

### 10.3 Withdrawal & Fees
- Bank transfer: free for the first 2 withdrawals/month; ₦50 flat after that
- Minimum payout: ₦500
- Same-day processing for amounts under ₦10,000 (via Paystack instant settlement)
- Larger amounts: next business day

## 15. Go-To-Market Strategy — UNILAG Launch

### Phase 0: Pre-Launch (Weeks 1–8)
- Recruit 10–15 Campus Champions (popular, trusted, active students across faculties)
- Run WhatsApp-based beta with 100 users — manual matching, observe behaviour
- Identify top 5 gig types by demand (data-driven category prioritisation)
- Build waitlist via landing page (current site)

### Phase 1: Soft Launch (Weeks 9–12)
- Open to waitlist: first 500 users get "Founding Hustler" badge permanently
- Each founding user gets ₦500 ZBD Credit to post their first gig free
- Subsidise first 100 gig completions (ZBD covers the transaction fee)
- Guerrilla marketing: flyers in hostels, noticeboards, departmental group chats

### Phase 2: Campus Penetration (Month 3–6)
- Faculty Reps programme: one ZBD ambassador per faculty
- Integration with student union channels (WhatsApp broadcast, announcements)
- "ZBD Week" — curated events, boosted gigs, prizes for top hustlers
- Press: student newspapers, campus radio, Nigerian student content creators

### Phase 3: Data Refinement (Month 6–12)
- Analyse gig completion rates by category, location, time of day
- Identify the top 20 most-posted gig types → feature them prominently
- Introduce ZBD Pro for the top 5% of power users
- Prepare expansion playbook for University 2

## 19. The Social Contract — ZBD's Promise

ZBD is not neutral. We are explicitly on the side of the student who is struggling.

We believe that **every student who is willing to work should be able to eat today.** Not next week when a job application is approved. Today.

We believe that **skills have no hierarchy.** Knowing how to wash plates fast, navigate campus queues, or find information quickly is as valuable as knowing Python. ZBD will reflect this in every design choice, every feature, every piece of copy.

We believe that **the wealth gap on campus is not destiny.** It is an inefficiency. And we are here to exploit that inefficiency — not for ourselves, but for the students who have always had something to offer, and simply never had a stage.

Zero Broke Days is not a company.

It is a movement that happens to have a business model.
